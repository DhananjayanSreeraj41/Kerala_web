Backwaters · Alappuzha

Alleppey

The Venice of the East

Alleppey is Kerala's backwater jewel — a slow, shimmering world of canals, paddy fields, and coconut groves where the pace of life is governed by water. Board a traditional houseboat and let the stillness wash over you.

What makes Alleppey special

🛶

Houseboat Stays

Spend the night on a Kettuvallam — a rice boat converted into a floating villa with bedrooms and a sun deck.

🚣

Nehru Trophy Boat Race

Held every August, this iconic snake boat race draws massive crowds across Vembanad Lake.

🌾

Kuttanad Paddy Fields

One of the few places on earth where farming occurs below sea level. Vast emerald-green paddies.

🐦

Bird Watching

Pathiramanal Island in Vembanad is a sanctuary for kingfishers, egrets, and Indian darters.

🌅

Sunset Cruises

Drift through canals at dusk as the sky turns amber and the water mirrors a thousand shades of gold.

🥥

Fresh Backwater Cuisine

Karimeen pollichathu in banana leaf, fresh prawns, and coconut-laced curries are the local staples.

Travel Tips

  • Best time: October to February. August is great for the Nehru Trophy Boat Race.
  • Book houseboats through ATDC for authentic, fair-priced options.
  • Hire a country boat for narrow canals — houseboats can't reach the prettiest spots.
  • Nearest airport: Cochin International Airport, 84 km away.

Hill Station · Idukki

Munnar

Where Tea Meets the Clouds

Munnar sits at 1,600 metres, draped in an endless quilt of tea gardens. The cool air carries the scent of cardamom, the slopes are an impossible shade of green, and misty mornings feel almost magical.

What makes Munnar special

🍵

Tea Estates & Factories

Walk through KDHP tea gardens and visit working factories to see the full journey from leaf to cup.

🌸

Neelakurinji Blooms

Once every 12 years, the hills turn violet with Neelakurinji flowers — a truly rare sight.

🦌

Eravikulam National Park

Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, with some of the best high-altitude trekking in South India.

💧

Attukal Waterfalls

Seasonal waterfalls that surge dramatically during monsoon, cutting through forested hillsides.

🌿

Spice Gardens

Guided tours through cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon gardens — the spices that put Kerala on the map.

🏕️

Top Station Viewpoint

At 1,880 m, sweeping views over the Tamil Nadu plains. On clear mornings visibility stretches for miles.

Travel Tips

  • Best time: September to May. Avoid July–August for landslide risk.
  • Carry a jacket — nights can drop to 8–10°C even in winter.
  • Buy fresh tea directly from the KDHP factory shop.
  • Nearest airport: Cochin International Airport, 130 km away.

Wildlife · Idukki

Thekkady

Into the Heart of Periyar

Thekkady is the gateway to Periyar Tiger Reserve — one of India's finest wildlife sanctuaries. Dense forests, spice plantations, and the shimmering Periyar Lake all teem with life.

What makes Thekkady special

🐘

Periyar Lake Boat Safari

Glide through the sanctuary as elephants wade in and tigers occasionally emerge at the bank.

🐯

Tiger Trail Trek

An overnight trek inside the reserve with trained tribal guides — Kerala's most immersive wildlife experience.

🌶️

Spice Village Tours

Kumily town is Kerala's spice capital. Walk through cardamom and pepper estates with local guides.

🎪

Kalaripayattu Shows

Watch Kerala's ancient martial art performed live — acrobatic, hypnotic, and thousands of years old.

🪵

Bamboo Rafting

Float through interior forest on bamboo rafts with naturalist guides. Serene and unforgettable.

🐦

Birdwatching

Over 260 species recorded, including Malabar Trogon, Great Hornbill, and the Indian Pitta.

Travel Tips

  • Book boat safaris early — the 7am slot offers the best wildlife sightings.
  • Tiger Trail permits must be booked weeks in advance through Kerala Forest Dept.
  • Best season: October to April.
  • Nearest airport: Madurai (Tamil Nadu), 140 km; Cochin, 190 km.

Beaches · Thiruvananthapuram

Kovalam

Kerala's Jewel Coast

Kovalam's three crescent beaches offer calm swimming waters, dramatic red cliff sunsets, and world-class Ayurvedic wellness centres all in one place.

What makes Kovalam special

🌊

Lighthouse Beach

The most popular stretch, guarded by a working red-and-white lighthouse. Safe swimming and a lively promenade.

🌿

Ayurveda Retreats

Kerala's highest concentration of authentic Ayurvedic resorts. 7–21 day Panchakarma programmes available.

🏄

Surfing

November to March brings consistent swells ideal for beginner and intermediate surfers.

🐟

Fresh Seafood

Choose your fish from the morning catch and have it grilled at a beach shack. Kerala masala fish fry is exceptional.

🏛️

Padmanabhaswamy Temple

17 km away — the wealthiest temple on earth, with stunning Dravidian architecture.

🌅

Vizhinjam Rock Cave

8th-century rock-cut cave temple carved into a cliff face near the beach. An unexpected ancient gem.

Travel Tips

  • Best season: October to March. Avoid monsoon — currents make swimming dangerous.
  • Red flags on the beach mean no swimming — always follow lifeguard warnings.
  • Book Ayurveda programmes with AYUSH-certified resorts to avoid scams.
  • Nearest airport: Trivandrum International Airport, just 13 km away.

Nature · Northern Kerala

Wayanad

The Green Sanctuary

Wayanad is Kerala's northern secret — evergreen forests, tribal hamlets, prehistoric cave temples, cascading waterfalls, and coffee-scented air. Less touristed than Munnar, more raw, more real.

What makes Wayanad special

🦋

Chembra Peak Trek

Kerala's highest trekking peak at 2,100 m, famous for the heart-shaped lake near the summit.

🏛️

Edakkal Caves

Neolithic rock carvings 6,000 years old, etched into cave walls above a mist-shrouded valley.

💦

Soochipara Waterfalls

A three-tiered waterfall plunging into a natural pool surrounded by dense forest.

☕

Coffee & Spice Estates

Wayanad produces some of India's finest coffee. Stay in a plantation homestay and wake to fresh bloom scents.

🐘

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

Part of one of South Asia's largest biosphere reserves. Elephant corridors cross the entire district.

🏡

Tribal Homestays

Stay with Kurichiya or Paniya tribal communities and learn about traditional forest knowledge and cooking.

Travel Tips

  • Best time: October to May. Monsoon makes trails slippery but forests look extraordinary.
  • Chembra Peak requires a forest department permit — get it at Meppadi range office early morning.
  • Best explored by car or bike — distances between attractions are significant.
  • Nearest airport: Calicut International Airport (Kozhikode), 85 km away.

Heritage · Ernakulam

Fort Kochi

Where Cultures Converge

Fort Kochi is Kerala's most cosmopolitan corner — a peninsula shaped by Arab, Portuguese, Dutch, and British presence. Cobbled lanes, colonial villas, ancient synagogues, Chinese fishing nets, and a world-class art scene all coexist here.

What makes Fort Kochi special

🎣

Chinese Fishing Nets

The iconic cantilevered nets silhouetted against Arabian Sea sunsets — the defining image of Kerala's coast.

⛪

St. Francis Church

India's oldest European church (1503), built by the Portuguese. Vasco da Gama was once buried here.

🎨

Kochi-Muziris Biennale

Asia's largest contemporary art festival, held every two years across Fort Kochi's heritage buildings.

🕍

Paradesi Synagogue

The oldest active synagogue in the Commonwealth (1568). Hand-painted Chinese tiles cover the floor.

🌶️

Spice Markets

The warehouses of Mattancherry still trade in pepper, cardamom, and cinnamon.

🎭

Kerala Folklore Museum

Three storeys housing over 4,000 artefacts representing Kerala's tribal, folk, and classical art heritage.

Travel Tips

  • Best explored on foot or by rented bicycle — lanes are narrow and distances are small.
  • Take the ferry from Ernakulam Jetty for a scenic 10-minute crossing.
  • Kathakali performances are held nightly at Kerala Kathakali Centre — book ahead in season.
  • Nearest airport: Cochin International Airport, 35 km away.
