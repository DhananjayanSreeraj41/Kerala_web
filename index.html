Backwaters · Alappuzha

Alleppey

The Venice of the East

Alleppey is Kerala's backwater jewel — a slow, shimmering world of canals, paddy fields, and coconut groves where the pace of life is governed by water. Board a traditional houseboat and let the stillness wash over you.

What makes Alleppey special

🛶

Houseboat Stays

Spend the night on a Kettuvallam — a rice boat converted into a floating villa with bedrooms and a sun deck.

🚣

Nehru Trophy Boat Race

Held every August, this iconic snake boat race draws massive crowds across Vembanad Lake.

🌾

Kuttanad Paddy Fields

One of the few places on earth where farming occurs below sea level. Vast emerald-green paddies.

🐦

Bird Watching

Pathiramanal Island in Vembanad is a sanctuary for kingfishers, egrets, and Indian darters.

🌅

Sunset Cruises

Drift through canals at dusk as the sky turns amber and the water mirrors a thousand shades of gold.

🥥

Fresh Backwater Cuisine

Karimeen pollichathu in banana leaf, fresh prawns, and coconut-laced curries are the local staples.

Travel Tips

  • Best time: October to February. August is great for the Nehru Trophy Boat Race.
  • Book houseboats through ATDC for authentic, fair-priced options.
  • Hire a country boat for narrow canals — houseboats can't reach the prettiest spots.
  • Nearest airport: Cochin International Airport, 84 km away.

Hill Station · Idukki

Munnar

Where Tea Meets the Clouds

Munnar sits at 1,600 metres, draped in an endless quilt of tea gardens. The cool air carries the scent of cardamom, the slopes are an impossible shade of green, and misty mornings feel almost magical.

What makes Munnar special

🍵

Tea Estates & Factories

Walk through KDHP tea gardens and visit working factories to see the full journey from leaf to cup.

🌸

Neelakurinji Blooms

Once every 12 years, the hills turn violet with Neelakurinji flowers — a truly rare sight.

🦌

Eravikulam National Park

Home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, with some of the best high-altitude trekking in South India.

💧

Attukal Waterfalls

Seasonal waterfalls that surge dramatically during monsoon, cutting through forested hillsides.

🌿

Spice Gardens

Guided tours through cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon gardens — the spices that put Kerala on the map.

🏕️

Top Station Viewpoint

At 1,880 m, sweeping views over the Tamil Nadu plains. On clear mornings visibility stretches for miles.

Travel Tips

  • Best time: September to May. Avoid July–August for landslide risk.
  • Carry a jacket — nights can drop to 8–10°C even in winter.
  • Buy fresh tea directly from the KDHP factory shop.
  • Nearest airport: Cochin International Airport, 130 km away.

Wildlife · Idukki

Thekkady

Into the Heart of Periyar

Thekkady is the gateway to Periyar Tiger Reserve — one of India's finest wildlife sanctuaries. Dense forests, spice plantations, and the shimmering Periyar Lake all teem with life.

What makes Thekkady special

🐘

Periyar Lake Boat Safari

Glide through the sanctuary as elephants wade in and tigers occasionally emerge at the bank.

🐯

Tiger Trail Trek

An overnight trek inside the reserve with trained tribal guides — Kerala's most immersive wildlife experience.

🌶️

Spice Village Tours

Kumily town is Kerala's spice capital. Walk through cardamom and pepper estates with local guides.

🎪

Kalaripayattu Shows

Watch Kerala's ancient martial art performed live — acrobatic, hypnotic, and thousands of years old.

🪵

Bamboo Rafting

Float through interior forest on bamboo rafts with naturalist guides. Serene and unforgettable.

🐦

Birdwatching

Over 260 species recorded, including Malabar Trogon, Great Hornbill, and the Indian Pitta.

Travel Tips

  • Book boat safaris early — the 7am slot offers the best wildlife sightings.
  • Tiger Trail permits must be booked weeks in advance through Kerala Forest Dept.
  • Best season: October to April.
  • Nearest airport: Madurai (Tamil Nadu), 140 km; Cochin, 190 km.

Beaches · Thiruvananthapuram

Kovalam

Kerala's Jewel Coast

Kovalam's three crescent beaches offer calm swimming waters, dramatic red cliff sunsets, and world-class Ayurvedic wellness centres all in one place.

What makes Kovalam special

🌊

Lighthouse Beach

The most popular stretch, guarded by a working red-and-white lighthouse. Safe swimming and a lively promenade.

🌿

Ayurveda Retreats

Kerala's highest concentration of authentic Ayurvedic resorts. 7–21 day Panchakarma programmes available.

🏄

Surfing

November to March brings consistent swells ideal for beginner and intermediate surfers.

🐟

Fresh Seafood

Choose your fish from the morning catch and have it grilled at a beach shack. Kerala masala fish fry is exceptional.

🏛️

Padmanabhaswamy Temple

17 km away — the wealthiest temple on earth, with stunning Dravidian architecture.

🌅

Vizhinjam Rock Cave

8th-century rock-cut cave temple carved into a cliff face near the beach. An unexpected ancient gem.

Travel Tips

  • Best season: October to March. Avoid monsoon — currents make swimming dangerous.
  • Red flags on the beach mean no swimming — always follow lifeguard warnings.
  • Book Ayurveda programmes with AYUSH-certified resorts to avoid scams.
  • Nearest airport: Trivandrum International Airport, just 13 km away.

Nature · Northern Kerala

Wayanad

The Green Sanctuary

Wayanad is Kerala's northern secret — evergreen forests, tribal hamlets, prehistoric cave temples, cascading waterfalls, and coffee-scented air. Less touristed than Munnar, more raw, more real.

What makes Wayanad special

🦋

Chembra Peak Trek

Kerala's highest trekking peak at 2,100 m, famous for the heart-shaped lake near the summit.

🏛️

Edakkal Caves

Neolithic rock carvings 6,000 years old, etched into cave walls above a mist-shrouded valley.

💦

Soochipara Waterfalls

A three-tiered waterfall plunging into a natural pool surrounded by dense forest.

☕

Coffee & Spice Estates

Wayanad produces some of India's finest coffee. Stay in a plantation homestay and wake to fresh bloom scents.

🐘

Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary

Part of one of South Asia's largest biosphere reserves. Elephant corridors cross the entire district.

🏡

Tribal Homestays

Stay with Kurichiya or Paniya tribal communities and learn about traditional forest knowledge and cooking.

Travel Tips

  • Best time: October to May. Monsoon makes trails slippery but forests look extraordinary.
  • Chembra Peak requires a forest department permit — get it at Meppadi range office early morning.
  • Best explored by car or bike — distances between attractions are significant.
  • Nearest airport: Calicut International Airport (Kozhikode), 85 km away.

Heritage · Ernakulam

Fort kochi

Where Cultures Converge

Fort Kochi is Kerala's most cosmopolitan corner — a peninsula shaped by Arab, Portuguese, Dutch, and British presence. Cobbled lanes, colonial villas, ancient synagogues, Chinese fishing nets, and a world-class art scene all coexist here.

What makes Fort Kochi special

🎣

Chinese Fishing Nets

The iconic cantilevered nets silhouetted against Arabian Sea sunsets — the defining image of Kerala's coast.

⛪

St. Francis Church

India's oldest European church (1503), built by the Portuguese. Vasco da Gama was once buried here.

🎨

Kochi-Muziris Biennale

Asia's largest contemporary art festival, held every two years across Fort Kochi's heritage buildings.

🕍

Paradesi Synagogue

The oldest active synagogue in the Commonwealth (1568). Hand-painted Chinese tiles cover the floor.

🌶️

Spice Markets

The warehouses of Mattancherry still trade in pepper, cardamom, and cinnamon.

🎭

Kerala Folklore Museum

Three storeys housing over 4,000 artefacts representing Kerala's tribal, folk, and classical art heritage.

Travel Tips

  • Best explored on foot or by rented bicycle — lanes are narrow and distances are small.
  • Take the ferry from Ernakulam Jetty for a scenic 10-minute crossing.
  • Kathakali performances are held nightly at Kerala Kathakali Centre — book ahead in season.
  • Nearest airport: Cochin International Airport, 35 km away.

Beaches · Thiruvananthapuram

Varkala

Cliffs, Coast & Calm

Varkala is Kerala's cliff-top coastal gem — dramatic laterite cliffs plunging into the Arabian Sea, a sacred beach, yoga retreats, and a laid-back vibe that draws travellers back year after year.

What makes Varkala special

🏖️

Papanasam Beach

A sacred beach believed to wash away sins. Unique for its mineral springs that flow directly onto the shore.

🧘

Yoga & Meditation

Varkala's clifftop is lined with yoga studios and wellness centres offering daily classes and retreats.

🌊

Cliff Walks

Walk the red laterite cliffs at sunset for panoramic Arabian Sea views that stretch to the horizon.

🛕

Janardhana Swami Temple

A 2,000-year-old Vishnu temple perched above the sea — one of Kerala's most sacred pilgrimage sites.

🐟

Fresh Seafood

Clifftop restaurants serve the morning's catch with views of the sea — lobster, tiger prawns, and Kerala fish curry.

🏄

Surfing & Swimming

The beach has calmer northern sections ideal for swimming, and surf schools for beginners during winter months.

Travel Tips

  • Best season: October to March. Avoid monsoon — sea conditions are dangerous.
  • The cliff path north of the beach has the best sunset views — arrive 30 minutes early.
  • Respect temple dress codes if visiting Janardhana Swami Temple.
  • Nearest airport: Trivandrum International Airport, 51 km away.

Cuisine · Kerala

Kerala Food

A Symphony of Spice & Coconut

Kerala's cuisine is one of India's most distinctive — built on coconut, curry leaves, black pepper, and fresh seafood. From grand Onam Sadhyas to street-side puttu, every meal is a reflection of the land itself.

Must-Try Kerala Dishes

🍌

Onam Sadhya

A ceremonial vegetarian feast of 26+ dishes served on a fresh banana leaf — the pinnacle of Kerala cooking.

🐟

Karimeen Pollichathu

Pearl spot fish marinated in spices and slow-cooked in banana leaf — a backwater region classic.

🥥

Puttu & Kadala Curry

Steamed rice-flour cylinders with black chickpea curry — Kerala's beloved everyday breakfast.

🍛

Kerala Prawn Curry

Tiger prawns simmered in a fiery coconut milk gravy with kudampuli (Malabar tamarind). Unmissable.

🫓

Appam & Stew

Lacy rice hoppers with a mild coconut vegetable or chicken stew — a Sunday morning ritual across Kerala.

🍮

Payasam

Rice or lentil pudding cooked in jaggery and coconut milk, garnished with cashews and raisins fried in ghee.

Food Travel Tips

  • For an authentic Sadhya, visit during Onam (August–September) or at a traditional Kerala restaurant.
  • The best seafood is found in small family-run restaurants near the coast — avoid tourist-facing menus.
  • Toddy shops (Kallu Shaap) serve incredibly fresh fish with local rice wine — a true local experience.
  • Thalassery in northern Kerala is known as the birthplace of Kerala's unique biryani — worth a detour.

Culture · Kerala

Festivals

Colour, Percussion & Devotion

Kerala's festivals are among the most spectacular in India — a fusion of temple ritual, community celebration, and artistic performance. The calendar is packed year-round with events that transform entire towns.

Kerala's Great Festivals

🐘

Thrissur Pooram

Kerala's grandest temple festival — 30 caparisoned elephants, competing percussion orchestras, and a legendary fireworks finale.

🌸

Onam

Kerala's harvest festival celebrated with flower carpets (Pookalam), boat races, and the grand Sadhya feast across 10 days.

🪔

Vishu

The Kerala New Year, marked by the auspicious Vishukkani — the first sight of the morning filled with gold and nature's abundance.

🚣

Nehru Trophy Boat Race

The world's most famous snake boat race — over 100 oarsmen per boat, held on Vembanad Lake every August.

🎭

Navaratri at Sabarimala

Millions of pilgrims trek through forest to the hilltop Ayyappa shrine — one of the world's largest annual pilgrimages.

🎪

Theyyam

A trance ritual art form from northern Kerala where performers embody deities through elaborate costume and dance.

Festival Travel Tips

  • Thrissur Pooram is held in April/May — book accommodation months in advance as it draws huge crowds.
  • Onam falls in August–September; Thiruvananthapuram and Thrissur host the best celebrations.
  • Theyyam season runs November to May in Kannur and Kasargod districts — deeply sacred, dress respectfully.
  • Photography is welcomed at most festivals but always ask permission near temple sanctuaries.

Nature · Kerala

Wildlife

Into Kerala's Wild Heart

Kerala's Western Ghats form one of the world's eight biodiversity hotspots. Three biosphere reserves, five national parks, and dozens of sanctuaries protect an extraordinary range of mammals, birds, and endemic species found nowhere else on earth.

Kerala's Wildlife Highlights

🐯

Periyar Tiger Reserve

Kerala's most famous wildlife destination — tigers, leopards, and wild elephants around the scenic Periyar Lake.

🦌

Nilgiri Tahr

The endangered mountain goat, found only in Eravikulam National Park's high grasslands above Munnar.

🐘

Wayanad Elephant Corridor

Wayanad connects to Nagarhole and Bandipur — one of South Asia's largest elephant population zones.

🦅

Silent Valley Birdlife

Silent Valley National Park shelters the Lion-tailed Macaque and over 200 bird species in untouched rainforest.

🐊

Crocodile & River Wildlife

Mugger crocodiles, otters, and freshwater turtles inhabit the rivers and backwater systems of central Kerala.

🦋

Butterfly Diversity

Kerala records over 330 butterfly species. The Ghats are one of Asia's premier butterfly-watching destinations.

Wildlife Travel Tips

  • Best season for wildlife sightings: October to April when animals gather near water sources.
  • Book forest department safaris and permits well in advance — quotas are strictly limited.
  • Dawn and dusk are prime sighting hours — choose early morning boat or jeep safaris.
  • Hire a certified naturalist guide — they dramatically improve sighting rates and know animal behaviour.

Coast · Kerala

Beaches

900 km of Arabian Sea Shore

Kerala's coastline is one of India's most varied — from the dramatic laterite cliffs of Varkala to the calm lagoon shores of Alleppey, the fishing villages of Kannur to the resort beaches of Kovalam. Each stretch has its own character.

Kerala's Best Beaches

🏖️

Kovalam Lighthouse Beach

Kerala's most famous beach — a safe, sheltered crescent with a working lighthouse and world-class Ayurveda resorts nearby.

🌊

Varkala Cliff Beach

Dramatic red laterite cliffs dropping to the sea, mineral springs on the shore, and a sacred temple above the waves.

🎣

Cherai Beach

A rare beach where the Arabian Sea and backwaters nearly meet. Dolphins are frequently spotted just offshore.

🐚

Kannur Beaches

Muzhappilangad, India's longest drive-in beach — 4 km of hard sand where vehicles are permitted on the shore.

🌴

Marari Beach

A quiet fishing village beach fringed by coconut palms — unhurried, pristine, and largely undiscovered by mass tourism.

🏄

Surfing at Varkala

November to March brings reliable swells. Varkala and Kovalam both have surf schools for all ability levels.

Beach Travel Tips

  • Best season: October to March. Monsoon brings dangerous currents — avoid swimming June to September.
  • Always swim near lifeguard posts — Kerala's beaches can have strong rip currents without warning.
  • For solitude, head north — Kannur and Kasargod district beaches are far less visited than Kovalam.
  • Early mornings offer the best experience — fishing boats returning, empty sands, and cool temperatures.
